Steps of Taxes for Rental Property in Spain

Income Tax on Rental Income

You must declare rental income and pay tax on it.

If you are a tax resident in Spain:

  • Rental income is included in your Personal Income Tax (IRPF).
  • Tax rates are progressive (19%â€“47%, depending on total income).
  • You can deduct allowable expenses such as:
    • Mortgage interest
    • Repairs and maintenance
    • Property management fees
    • Utilities (if paid by you)
    • Local taxes related to the property

If you are a non-resident:

  • Rental income is taxed at a flat rate of 24% for non-EU residents (19% for EU/EEA residents).
  • You cannot deduct as many expenses as residents, except for mortgage interest (and only if you are from the EU/EEA).

Property Ownership Taxes

Regardless of residency, property owners in Spain must pay:

  1. IBI (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles)
    • Annual municipal property tax based on cadastral value.
    • Usually around 0.4%â€“1.1% of cadastral value.
  2. Wealth Tax (Impuesto sobre el Patrimonio)
    • Applies if your net assets exceed a certain threshold (varies by region, usually ~â‚¬700,000 excluding primary residence).

  • If you rent out your property short-term for tourism, there may be additional VAT (IVA) or special tourism regulations.
