3. Financial Support

+

Non-Resident Individual Mortgage

+

Foreign individuals (non-residents) can get a mortgage in Spain.

Here's what to expect:

Maximum Loan-to-Value (LTV)

Typically 60%–70% of the property's purchase price or appraised value (compared to up to 80% for residents). This means you usually need at least 30%–40% down payment.

Interest Rates

Usually slightly higher than for residents, often between 3%–6%, depending on the bank and your financial profile.

Term

Usually up to 20–25 years.

Requirements
  • Passport or EU/EEA ID
  • NIE (Número de Identificación de Extranjero — foreigner ID number)
  • Proof of income and assets (e.g., bank statements, employment contract, tax returns)
  • Credit history check (sometimes in your home country)
  • Spanish bank account

💡 Some banks require you to also have Spanish residency, but many allow mortgages for non-residents if you meet the criteria.

Foreign Company Mortgage

+

Foreign companies can apply for a mortgage in Spain, but it is more complicated:

Purpose of Purchase
  • If it's for commercial or investment purposes (e.g., renting property), a company structure is more common.
  • If it's for private use, banks prefer individuals.
Loan Terms
  • Usually 50%–60% LTV, sometimes less.
  • Interest rates may be higher.
  • Shorter loan terms (often 15–20 years).
Requirements
  • Company registration documents (translated into Spanish)
  • Proof of economic activity
  • Financial statements
  • Spanish tax identification for the company
  • Sometimes a Spanish-based partner or representative
Costs
  • Additional legal and notary costs
  • Company maintenance costs
  • Annual corporate tax obligations

Income Tax on Rental Income

+

You must declare rental income and pay tax on it.

If you are a tax resident in Spain:
  • Rental income is included in your Personal Income Tax (IRPF).
  • Tax rates are progressive (19%–47%, depending on total income).
  • You can deduct allowable expenses such as:
    • Mortgage interest
    • Repairs and maintenance
    • Property management fees
    • Utilities (if paid by you)
    • Local taxes related to the property
If you are a non-resident:
  • Rental income is taxed at a flat rate of 24% for non-EU residents (19% for EU/EEA residents).
  • You cannot deduct as many expenses as residents, except for mortgage interest (and only if you are from the EU/EEA).

Property Ownership Taxes

+

Regardless of residency, property owners in Spain must pay:

IBI (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles)
  • Annual municipal property tax based on cadastral value.
  • Usually around 0.4%–1.1% of cadastral value.
Wealth Tax (Impuesto sobre el Patrimonio)
  • Applies if your net assets exceed a certain threshold (varies by region, usually ~€700,000 excluding primary residence).
  • Tax rates: 0.2%–2.5%.
Plusvalía Municipal (Capital Gains on Sale)
  • Paid when you sell the property; calculated based on increase in cadastral value.

VAT or Tourist Rental Considerations

+
  • If you rent out your property short-term for tourism, there may be additional VAT (IVA) or special tourism regulations.
  • If you rent long-term, normal rental income rules apply (no VAT).

💡 Example: If you are a resident, rent your property long-term for €12,000/year, and have €2,000 in allowable expenses, your taxable rental income is €10,000. This gets added to your other income and taxed according to your IRPF bracket. If you are a non-resident, you pay 24% of €12,000 = €2,880, without most expense deductions.

Tricks & tips

  • Save time and be quick at the toll gate on AP7 high way without waiting time and apply for a token at your Bank. This plastic tool will open the toll gate automatically and payment is connected to your Spanish bank account, and the amount will be deducted monthly.
  • Find the best rate for your electricity rate and change your Electricity supplier even monthly, if you like.
  • TAX needs to be paid for non-residents yearly without any renting income as well.
  • Percentage of the Property tax depends on type of property. It is resale property and land (IVA 7%) or new sale (IVA 10%) property.
  • Local markets (clothes, vegetables & fruits & local products etc.) are in each city on different days, weekly.
  • Siesta time is between 2-5 PM, when mostly the stores and restaurants are closed.
  • Supermarkets are closed on Sundays but during the summer season they are opened (July, August).
  • Apply for your Digital Certificate and manage your official process simple, faster online. You need NIE first.
  • Often, banks will view foreign companies as higher risk, and lending conditions will be stricter. You may also face extra scrutiny from Spanish tax authorities. Key tip for property purchases: many foreigners prefer applying as individuals rather than through a company unless there’s a strong tax or business reason, because it is simpler and cheaper.
