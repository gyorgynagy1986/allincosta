Financial support in Spain

+

1. Non-Resident Individual Mortgage

+

Foreign individuals (non-residents) can get a mortgage in Spain.

Maximum Loan-to-Value (LTV up to 70%)

Interest Rates

usually slightly higher than for residents, often between 3%–6%.

Term

Usually up to 20–25 years.

Requirements

  • Passport or EU/EEA ID
  • NIE (Número de Identificación de Extranjero — foreign ID number)
  • Proof of income and assets (e.g., bank statements, employment contract, tax returns)
  • Credit history check (sometimes in your home country)
  • Spanish bank account

2. Foreign Company Mortgage

+

Foreign companies can apply for a mortgage in Spain, but it is more complicated:

Purpose of Purchase

Loan Terms

  • Usually 50%–60% LTV, sometimes less.
  • Interest rates may be higher.
  • Shorter loan terms (often 15–20 years).

Requirements

  • Company registration documents (translated into Spanish)
  • Proof of economic activity
  • Financial statements
  • Spanish tax identification for the company
  • Sometimes a Spanish-based partner or representative

Costs

  • Additional legal and notary costs
  • Company maintenance costs
  • Annual corporate tax obligations
